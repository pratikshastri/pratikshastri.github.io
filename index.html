ITCS 2026 Noncommutative circuits Lower bounds

Lower Bounds for Noncommutative Circuits with Low Syntactic Degree

Pratik Shastri

DOI PDF
Abstract

Proving lower bounds on the size of noncommutative arithmetic circuits is an important problem in arithmetic circuit complexity. For explicit $n$ variate polynomials of degree $\Theta(n)$, the best known general bound is $\Omega(n \log n)$. Recent work of Chatterjee and Hrubeš has provided stronger $(\Omega(n^2))$ bounds for the restricted class of homogeneous circuits. This paper extends these results to a broader class of circuits by using syntactic degree as a complexity measure.

FSTTCS 2025 ROABPs NP-hardness

On the Hardness of Order Finding and Equivalence Testing for ROABPs

Joint work with C. Ramya

DOI PDF
Abstract

The complexity of representing a polynomial by a Read-Once Oblivious Algebraic Branching Program (ROABP) is highly dependent on the chosen variable ordering. Building on prior hardness results, we prove that approximating the minimum ROABP width up to any constant factor is NP-hard when the input is presented as a circuit, and show NP-hardness for affine $\mathrm{GL}_n$ orbit membership of width-$w$ ROABPs.

RANDOM 2025 PIT Nonassociative algebras

Efficient Polynomial Identity Testing Over Nonassociative Algebras

Joint work with C. Ramya and Partha Mukhopadhyay

DOI
Abstract

We design efficient polynomial identity testing algorithms over the nonassociative polynomial algebra, where multiplication among formal variables is commutative but not associative. The results include randomized black-box PIT algorithms, deterministic white-box identity testing, and quasipolynomial-size hitting sets for small-depth circuits.

ITCS 2024 Planar circuits Lower bounds

Lower Bounds for Planar Arithmetic Circuits

Joint work with C. Ramya

DOI
Abstract

We study planar arithmetic circuits and prove an $\Omega(n\log n)$ lower bound on the size of planar arithmetic circuits computing explicit bilinear forms on $2n$ variables. The work also gives stronger read-once planar lower bounds and fine separations among planar models of computation.

arXiv 2026 Noncommutative circuits Lower bounds

A Quadratic Lower Bound for Noncommutative Circuits

Pratik Shastri

arXiv PDF
Abstract

We prove that every fan-in $2$ noncommutative arithmetic circuit computing the palindrome polynomial has size $\Omega(n^2)$. The proof builds on and refines a previous work of the author. The new ingredients in the proof were generated by Gemini 3.1 Pro.
